Diamonds Are Forever

Released in 1971, Sean Connery returns as Bond, investigating a diamond smuggling operation which leads him to his arch-enemy Blofeld. The plot involves using diamonds to create a laser satellite.

Live and Let Die

Released in 1973, Roger Moore's first Bond film, where he investigates the mysterious deaths of three MI6 agents, leading him to a Harlem drug lord, Mr. Big, who is planning to distribute heroin for free to increase drug addiction.

The Man with the Golden Gun

Released in 1974, Bond is pitted against a professional assassin, Francisco Scaramanga, who charges $1 million per kill. Bond must retrieve a device that can convert solar energy into a weapon of mass destruction.
